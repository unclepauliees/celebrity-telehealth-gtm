Naming Risk — Red Flag

Whatever name is finalized — it must not inadvertently imply clinical claims or superiority to FDA-approved treatments. Given the regulatory climate post-2026 enforcement surge (30 warning letters in March 2026 alone), anything that sounds like "we cure" or "we guarantee" is a liability magnet. COMMONGROUND signals community, access, and collective ownership — not clinical promises. That is the right lane. Every celebrity owner's social content must carry FTC-compliant ownership disclosures. The brand is legally responsible for any claim its equity holders make on its behalf.

Executive Summary

The Thesis

Company Thesis: This joint venture pairs the most trusted cultural voices in Black and Latino America with the most battle-tested white-label telehealth infrastructure on the market — OpenLoop Health — to build the first celebrity-equity-owned, community-anchored telehealth network designed specifically for underserved patients who have historically been ignored by mainstream healthcare brands.

The Cultural Opportunity: 21% of Black adults and 18% of Hispanic adults turn to social media influencers for health information, versus 12% of White adults. That is not a bug in the system — it is the system telling us where trust actually lives. When you align clinical infrastructure with the cultural credibility of Ye, LeBron, Rihanna, and Cardi B — artists whose audiences already make health decisions based on their signal — you are not running a marketing campaign. You are engineering a trust transfer at scale.

Why Now (2026 Market Timing):

  • The telehealth market hits $22.8B in 2024 and is tracking toward $156.3B by 2035 (19.15% CAGR)
  • GLP-1 is the fastest-growing telehealth category
  • The SPEAK Act (February 2026) now mandates language access in telehealth — opening a legally-privileged lane for LEP-first brands
  • The MAHA initiative redirects federal dollars toward chronic disease prevention
  • Competitors Hims & Hers are down 47% YoY under FDA pressure; WeightWatchers filed for bankruptcy
  • The category is reshuffling in real time. First-mover cultural positioning advantage closes within 18 months
North Star Metric

2,000 activated patients across all four sub-brands, with LeBron sub-brand achieving 500 patients in beta by Day 35 and full ecosystem live by Day 45. Secondary north star: zero FTC enforcement actions or FDA warning letters in the launch window.

1.3 OpenLoop as "Invisible Infrastructure"

OpenLoop is the engine. COMMONGROUND is the dashboard. The patient never sees OpenLoop — they see Cardi B's sub-brand, or LeBron's. This is the strategic advantage.

Why "Invisible" is the right architecture
  • Patients in underserved communities have documented distrust of institutional healthcare brands. A "Powered by OpenLoop" badge on a patient intake form is a liability, not an asset, in this demographic context.
  • OpenLoop's January 2026 data breach is a known event. Burying OpenLoop's brand in the B2B layer — while aggressively communicating COMMONGROUND's patient data commitments at the consumer layer — is the cleanest trust recovery strategy.
  • Celebrity brand integrity depends on the consumer believing the experience is theirs, not a white-labeled product. OpenLoop's white-label architecture was purpose-built for exactly this scenario — they power 170+ organizations invisibly today.
OpenLoop Infrastructure Assets Being Activated
  • 20,000+ clinicians; 50-state coverage; 30+ digital health specialties
  • NCQA-accredited Credentialing Verification Organization
  • 600+ payer plans covering ~250 million patient lives (Medicare and Medicaid included)
  • 100+ AI clinical pathways — vetted protocols deployed immediately
  • Season Health acquisition (2026) — enables GLP-1 + nutrition bundling for weight loss sub-brands
  • 7-week average onboarding time from contract to go-live
  • Revenue cycle management handled end-to-end

2.1  ·  Ye (Kanye West)
YZY HEALTH™

"The mental health brand that doesn't ask you to be someone else — because you're already enough."

Target Audience

Black males ages 18-35; creative professionals; Yeezy loyalists; men who have publicly or privately resisted mental health treatment due to stigma. Psychographic: "I'm not weak, I just carry a lot."

Hero Health Category

Behavioral & Mental Health — telepsychiatry, therapy access, medication-assisted care

Secondary Categories

Sleep health, stress management, men's primary care

Content & Trust Strategy

Ye's relationship with mental health is not a liability — it is the most authentic credential in this category. His public experience with bipolar disorder diagnosis, creative episodes, and public scrutiny creates a permission structure to talk about mental health that no other celebrity can claim. Content strategy centers on destigmatization through creative expression — music, art, visual drops that reframe mental health as a creative practice, not a clinical intervention. Clinician intro content features Black male psychiatrists who speak Ye's language, not institutional language.

Cultural Risk Assessment HIGH

Kanye West is the highest-controversy asset in this portfolio. Risk vectors: antisemitic statements (2022-2024 public record), erratic public behavior, history of non-compliant conduct with business partners, and existing reputational rehabilitation uncertainty. The "Yeezy Health" concept has been floated in market research but has no established brand equity.

Mitigation Protocol
  • YZY HEALTH launches last in the 45-day sequence (Day 43-45), after infrastructure is proven
  • No Ye likeness in clinical or claims-bearing content — creative direction only
  • Separate content compliance review layer (48-hour hold on all Ye-adjacent posts)
  • Legal team pre-clears all Ye statements before they are amplified by brand channels
  • Controversy clause with 30-day trigger (shortened from standard 90-day due to elevated risk profile)
  • Brand architecture allows YZY HEALTH to operate independently — full brand quarantine is possible without shutting down other sub-brands
Equity Commitment Terms

6% equity; vesting contingent on quarterly "Wellness Drop" content (audio, visual, or written piece centered on mental health); 1 community mental health activation per quarter. No press requirement — Ye's content is his equity contribution.

Launch Activation Concept — Day 43-45

A private, invite-only listening session / mental health conversation — not a concert, not a press event. 50 Black men from Ye's creative community, 5 Black psychiatrists, no cameras except one documentary crew under NDA. The film drops 30 days later as the launch asset.

KING HEALTH™

"Built for people who perform at their highest level — and everyone who's trying to."

Target Audience

Black males 25-50; fathers; athletes (amateur to elite); men who see health as performance optimization, not medical weakness. Secondary: Black families through LeBron's family-brand equity. Psychographic: "I invest in everything I do."

Hero Health Category

Medical Weight Management + Men's Primary Care — GLP-1 management, metabolic health, performance labs

Secondary Categories

Pediatric behavioral health, cardiovascular health, RPM for chronic disease management

Content & Trust Strategy

LeBron is the most institutionally credible asset in this portfolio. SpringHill Company, Uninterrupted, Liverpool FC ownership, Klutch Sports — he has demonstrated that he can build businesses, not just endorse them. Content strategy: LeBron as patient zero — transparent documentation of his own health optimization journey (labs, nutrition, sleep, performance metrics). Clinician collaboration with sports medicine and performance health specialists. Uninterrupted distribution channel for long-form health content reaches the exact demographic (Black professional men 25-50) that is most underserved and most monetizable.

LeBron is the most brand-safe asset in this portfolio. Primary risks: NBA association (healthcare marketing rules during season), potential FTC scrutiny on equity-ownership disclosure (he is an owner, not an endorser — the disclosure language must be precise), and audience expectation gap (his fans expect elite athlete content; clinical primary care may feel ordinary).

"The King Lab" — LeBron releases a full health panel (blood work, metabolic markers, sleep data) on Uninterrupted, then books a telehealth consult with a KING HEALTH clinician on camera. No scripted dialogue. Real intake. Real results. It becomes the brand's origin story.

CARDI CARE™

"No gatekeeping. No judgment. Just real healthcare for real people."

Target Audience

Latina women (Dominican, Puerto Rican, Caribbean heritage); Black women; working-class and middle-class women ages 20-40 who have been ignored or condescended to by the healthcare system. Psychographic: "I know what I need. I just need someone to actually listen."

Hero Health Category

Mental Health + Women's Primary Care — anxiety, depression, ADHD, and the systemic healthcare barriers faced by Latina and Black women

Secondary Categories

Reproductive health, GLP-1 weight management, pediatric care access for single-parent households

Content & Trust Strategy

Cardi B's superpower is radical authenticity at massive scale. She is among the most-followed people on Instagram and TikTok. She has spoken publicly about postpartum depression, healthcare system failures, and the particular exhaustion of being a working-class Latina navigating institutions not built for her. She does not need a content script — she needs a compliance guardrail and a clinician she trusts. Content strategy: Cardi goes live with a CARDI CARE clinician, unscripted. Patient community stories — "real patients, real results" — featuring Latina and Black women from the Bronx, Houston, and Miami. Spanish-language content is a primary output, not an afterthought (SPEAK Act alignment).

Cultural Risk Assessment MEDIUM

Cardi B's authenticity is her brand moat, but it is also her risk vector. Her directness can conflict with FDA claim substantiation requirements — she may describe a clinical result in a way that constitutes an off-label claim or testimonial that triggers FTC scrutiny. Past legal history (prior criminal charges, assault case) is a known public record variable. The Offset relationship dynamic creates periodic tabloid risk that can bleed into brand cycle.

Mitigation Protocol
  • All Cardi B unscripted content (live streams, Stories, etc.) requires same-day compliance review before any brand amplification
  • Trained compliance liaison on Cardi's team with authority to flag content before it is retweeted or reposted by CARDI CARE accounts
  • Spanish-language content has a separate clinical translation review — medical terminology accuracy is non-negotiable
  • Brand architecture allows CARDI CARE to operate independently in a quarantine scenario
Equity Commitment Terms

6% equity. Wellness Commitment: 1 Spanish-language health education live stream per month, 1 Bronx/NYC community health event per quarter, TikTok content targeting Gen Z Latina health discovery (minimum 2 posts per month tagged for social search indexing).

Launch Activation Concept — Day 41-43

"Cardi Checks In" — a multi-part TikTok and Instagram series where Cardi B documents getting her annual health panel, has a telehealth consult for a real health concern (not scripted), and then runs a 48-hour open Q&A with a CARDI CARE clinician answering her community's health questions in real time. Fully bilingual: English and Spanish simultaneously.

Regulatory Compliance Framework

Compliance Priority — Read Before Any Content Goes Live

5.1 FDA Advertising Guardrails

Non-negotiable prohibitions for all sub-brand content
  • No "sameness" claims for compounded GLP-1 medications (FDA issued 30 warning letters in March 2026 — this is an active enforcement priority)
  • No miracle or outcome-guarantee language: "lose X pounds in Y days," "cure," "eliminate," "reverse"
  • No off-label claims for any FDA-approved drug
  • Celebrity testimonials about personal health results require mandatory disclaimer: "Individual results may vary. Not medical advice. Consult a clinician."
  • All before/after imagery requires substantiation file maintained by Medical Review Committee
GLP-1 Specific — KING HEALTH, FENTY WELLNESS, CARDI CARE
  • Compounded semaglutide/tirzepatide marketing must reflect current FDA shortage status in real time

Every celebrity — Ye, LeBron, Rihanna, Cardi B — is a co-owner of the brand they are promoting. This is not a standard endorsement. Under FTC guidelines (reference: FTC v. NextMed order, 2025), material connection must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously in every piece of commercial content.

6.1 The Trust Transfer Model

The core commercial thesis of this business is a trust transfer. It works in four steps:

Cultural Capital Accumulation

LeBron, Rihanna, Cardi B, and Ye have each spent decades building authentic relationships with audiences that are predominantly Black and Latinx. That relationship is not transactional — it is emotional, identity-based, and durable.

Equity Signal

Unlike ambassadors or endorsers, these artists own this company. Ownership changes the signal fundamentally. When Cardi B tells her audience about CARDI CARE, she is promoting her own company. Audiences can smell the difference.

Clinical Credibility Bridge

The first time a patient has a genuinely good telehealth experience through a celebrity-branded platform, the celebrity's trust capital transfers to clinical credibility. The doctor becomes trustworthy because the platform is trustworthy because the celebrity is trustworthy.

Community Lock-in

At scale, the platform stops being a celebrity brand and becomes a community institution. The celebrity is the door. The clinician relationship is what keeps the patient.
