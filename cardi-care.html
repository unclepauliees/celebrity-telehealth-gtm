SPEAK Act Alignment — February 2026

The SPEAK Act mandates language access in telehealth for Limited English Proficiency (LEP) patients. CARDI CARE is the most SPEAK Act-aligned sub-brand in the portfolio by design. Spanish as a primary (not translated) content and care language. All patient intake materials, consent forms, and clinical communications in English and Spanish simultaneously. Target: 30% of CARDI CARE patients are LEP patients at Day 90. This unlocks a payer pool that most telehealth brands have not touched.

Clinical Architecture

Health Categories

Hero Category
Behavioral / Mental Health

Cardi's public discussion of postpartum depression has already opened the door. Behavioral health as primary care — not specialty psychiatry as entry point. Anxiety, depression, postpartum, stress management. Destigmatization through Cardi's authentic voice is the clinical conversion mechanism.

Hero Category
Reproductive Health

Contraception, STI screening, cervical cancer screening, family planning. The highest-access-gap reproductive health category for Latina and Black women under 40. Telehealth removes geographic and cultural barriers to reproductive care access.

Secondary Category
Women's Primary Care

Annual exams, preventive screenings, chronic condition management (diabetes, hypertension — disproportionate in Latina women). Bilingual intake is the core differentiator. A Latina woman should never again have to describe her symptoms in a language she doesn't think in.

Pediatric Telehealth

Cardi's maternal identity (three children, public postpartum experience) extends naturally to pediatric access. Bilingual pediatric intake. Parents who are LEP patients need pediatric care in their primary language too. Minor acute care, developmental screening, vaccine consultations.

FTC / FDA Compliance

Legal Architecture

Mandatory FTC Disclosure — All Commercial Content

"Cardi B is a co-owner of CARDI CARE and receives equity compensation. This content reflects her genuine experience and does not constitute medical advice."

[Spanish] "Cardi B es copropietaria de CARDI CARE y recibe compensación en acciones. Este contenido refleja su experiencia genuina y no constituye consejo médico."

  • Trained compliance liaison on Cardi's team — authority to flag content before it is retweeted or reposted by CARDI CARE accounts. This is the single most important structural protection given Cardi's posting volume and spontaneity.
  • Behavioral health content: destigmatization framing approved. "Support," "talk to someone," "you don't have to handle this alone." Not "treats depression" or "cures anxiety."
  • Live content protocol: clinical staff monitors all live sessions with authority to privately message a stop-flag to the clinician if a claim emerges that requires real-time correction
  • Spanish-language content: certified medical interpreters translate all clinical materials — not automated translation. Liability exposure from a mistranslated consent form is existential.
  • All CARDI CARE bilingual content: reviewed by both English and Spanish compliance leads before publication
  • Postpartum content: reviewed by Board-certified psychiatrist before publication. Any mention of medication (including postpartum antidepressants) requires standard disclaimer language.
  • SPEAK Act compliance documentation: maintain LEP patient records per federal guidelines; language access plan filed with clinical compliance officer
Risk: Spontaneous Off-Platform Claims

Cardi B posts at volume and spontaneity that no other celebrity in this portfolio matches. The compliance risk is not a single launch event — it is the ongoing liability of a high-volume, authentic poster who owns equity in a healthcare company. Mitigation: dedicated compliance liaison with a retainer structure, clear escalation protocol, and explicit contractual language that Cardi's team will run CARDI CARE brand amplification through compliance before repost. This is the most operationally complex compliance relationship in the JV.
