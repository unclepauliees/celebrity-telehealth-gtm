Brand Identity

The Brand

Brand Positioning: FENTY WELLNESS is luxury-accessible women's health — healthcare reframed as a ritual of self-ownership rather than a clinical obligation. The brand sits at the intersection of the Fenty Beauty ethos (radical inclusivity, editorial quality, culture-shifting) and the reality that Black women and women of the diaspora are systematically underdiagnosed, underheard, and undertreated by mainstream healthcare.

Rihanna has built three global brand empires — Fenty Beauty, Savage X Fenty, Fenty Maison — each time redefining inclusivity in a category that excluded most of the world. FENTY WELLNESS applies the same logic to healthcare: if healthcare brands were designed for women who look like us, sound like us, and have our health history, what would they look like?

Voice & Tone

Voice archetype: Your brilliant best friend who happens to be an OB-GYN. Warm but precise. Unapologetically Black and Caribbean. Never clinical, never condescending. She's been in the room when the system failed you — and she is done with it.

  • ✓ Say: "This is your health. Own it."
  • ✗ Never: Condescension. "For women just like you." Infantilizing health education.
  • ✓ Say: "Healthcare as editorial. The ritual of being well."
  • ✗ Never: Before/after imagery. Weight-loss framing. Anxiety-based health marketing.

Brand Personality

The Fenty Brand Extension Thesis

The risk and the opportunity are the same thing. Fenty is already a global brand trust signal. The risk is brand overextension — healthcare is a significant pivot from beauty and fashion, and a clinical underperformance reflects on the masterbrand. The opportunity is that Fenty's brand thesis (radical inclusivity, unapologetic quality for everyone the industry ignored) is exactly the thesis that women's healthcare is missing. The deal: FENTY WELLNESS must be clinically excellent to protect the brand. The return: if it is, Rihanna becomes the only celebrity who has redefined three industries — music, beauty, and healthcare.

Clinical Architecture

Health Categories

Hero Category
Women's Health / Hormone

Hormonal health, menstrual health, PCOS, perimenopause, thyroid. The most underfunded, underresearched area of women's healthcare — and the one Rihanna's audience is most vocal about needing access to. Black women are disproportionately diagnosed late with fibroid disease and PCOS.

Hero Category
Maternal Health

Prenatal, postnatal, and interconception care. Black maternal mortality crisis is the primary clinical imperative. Clinician roster: Black and Caribbean-heritage OB-GYNs and maternal health specialists. Telehealth prenatal visits dramatically increase access for Black women in maternal health deserts.

Secondary Category
Dermatology

Skin health for darker skin tones — historically undertreated in dermatology, which was founded and largely practiced without reference to melanated skin. Hyperpigmentation, chronic skin conditions, melanoma screening (rising in Black populations). Fenty Beauty audience crossover is direct.

Secondary Category
Nutrition & Weight (GLP-1)

Via OpenLoop's Season Health partnership. Framed as metabolic health and nutrition optimization — not weight loss clinic positioning. GLP-1 access for women with hormonal weight factors. FENTY WELLNESS is explicitly not a diet brand; this protocol is tucked behind a full metabolic health workup.

Tertiary Category
Mental Health / Anxiety

Women's mental health with intersectional framing — racial stress, caregiving burden, postpartum depression. Not positioned as psychiatry but as emotional health and support. Feed into YZY HEALTH referral pipeline for complex psychiatric needs.

Language Access
Caribbean Creole + Spanish

Caribbean Creole support (Haitian Creole priority) for diaspora patients. SPEAK Act compliance. Partnership with community health translators in Caribbean and Haitian diaspora communities in Miami, New York, Boston. Target: 15% of FENTY WELLNESS patients as LEP patients by Day 90.

Content & Trust Strategy

The Content System

The FENTY WELLNESS content strategy applies Fenty Beauty's most important insight: the category had been designed for someone else, and that someone else was tired of it. Healthcare content for Black women has been either clinical and alienating, or wellness-adjacent and vague. FENTY WELLNESS occupies neither lane. It is editorial. It is precise. It is made for the person who has been dismissed in a doctor's office and decided to be her own advocate.

01

The Fenty Check — Launch Campaign

Rihanna documents her first FENTY WELLNESS telehealth consultation — annual exam, hormone panel, full intake. Shot to Fenty Beauty editorial standards: cinematic, intimate, zero clinical sterility. The message: your checkup is not a chore. It is a ritual of ownership. Campaign visual language is immediately recognizable as Fenty — the healthcare category has never seen content that looks like this.

02

Black & Caribbean Women Clinician Series

FENTY WELLNESS clinicians — Black women OB-GYNs, endocrinologists, maternal health specialists — appear in editorial-quality short-form content talking about what they see every week, what the research says about Black women's health gaps, and what a patient should expect when she shows up to a FENTY WELLNESS visit. Trust through visibility: these clinicians are the brand.

03

Patient Story Series — "She Showed Up"

HIPAA-compliant patient stories featuring real women — ages 22 to 55, Black, Caribbean, Latina — who used FENTY WELLNESS to address a health concern the mainstream system had dismissed or delayed. Not outcomes-focused testimonials. Narrative-focused: the decision to show up, the experience, the feeling of being heard. Distributed via Rihanna's Instagram and Fenty social channels.

04

Maternal Health Awareness Partnership

Launch in partnership with the Black Women's Health Imperative (BWHI) or Black Mamas Matter Alliance — aligning FENTY WELLNESS with established maternal health advocacy infrastructure. Press event, joint content, community health activation. This positions FENTY WELLNESS as structurally committed to maternal health equity, not just marketing to it.

05

Fenty Beauty Cross-Audience Activation

The Fenty Beauty database is the most valuable customer overlap in this portfolio. Fenty Beauty customers are pre-qualified: they trust Rihanna's product curation, they are already spending on self-care, and they skew toward the exact demographic FENTY WELLNESS serves. Email and social cross-promotion from Fenty Beauty to FENTY WELLNESS — with explicit messaging: "Rihanna's wellness brand." Subject line: "You take care of your skin. What about everything else?"

FTC / FDA Compliance

Legal Architecture

Mandatory FTC Disclosure — All Commercial Content

"Rihanna is a co-owner of FENTY WELLNESS and receives equity compensation. This content reflects her genuine experience and does not constitute medical advice."

  • Maternal health content: highest clinical review threshold. All prenatal claims reviewed by Board-certified OB-GYN before publication. Zero implied outcomes for pregnancy or fetal health.
  • Hormone health content: no claims of treating PCOS, endometriosis, or other diagnosed conditions without substantiation. "Support" and "manage" — not "cure" or "treat."
  • Fenty Brand Extension: formal brand protection protocol between FENTY WELLNESS and Fenty Beauty LLC to prevent consumer confusion, set quality standards, and protect Fenty masterbrand in event of adverse clinical outcome.
  • GLP-1 protocols: zero compounding claims. No before/after imagery. Metabolic framing only — not diet or weight-loss marketing.
  • All Rihanna content: 48-hour Medical Review Committee clearance before publication on any commercial channel
  • Patient testimonials: HIPAA-compliant consent, narrative focus (not outcomes), reviewed by legal and clinical compliance before release
  • Caribbean Creole / Spanish content: translated by certified medical interpreters, not automated translation
Critical Risk: Brand Overextension

Fenty is the most established masterbrand in this portfolio. A clinical failure or FTC action does not only harm FENTY WELLNESS — it harms Fenty Beauty and Savage X Fenty. This is the highest-stakes compliance scenario in the JV. Mitigation: FENTY WELLNESS must have the most rigorous Medical Review Committee in the portfolio, and Rihanna's team must have explicit veto authority over any clinical content that has not been cleared by that committee.
