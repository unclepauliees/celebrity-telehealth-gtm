01 Brand Foundation

The fixed core. Every decision downstream — visual, verbal, structural — is a consequence of what is defined here. Read it as constraint, not suggestion.

1.1

Brand Positioning Statement

One paragraph. No revision cycles. Carved.

Obsidian Capital Partners is the principal that stays. In a capital market defined by intermediaries who route transactions and depart, we take ownership positions in infrastructure and industrial platform companies — structuring capital, building management capacity, and compounding value across cycles measured in years, not quarters. We do not advise from a distance. We do not optimize for transaction volume. We select a small number of businesses where structural advantage is buildable, commit our capital and our time, and remain until the architecture we designed has been tested by the market. This is not a service offering. It is a building orientation — and the discipline to hold it.

1.2

Brand Architecture

Three tiers. One parent. Defined relationships, not improvised ones.

Tier 01 — Master Brand
Obsidian Capital Partners
The institutional parent. All authority, all credibility, all accountability flows upward to this name. Never subordinated to a platform brand in any investor, public, or partner context. Controls palette, typography, structural design language.
Tier 02 — Platform Brands
Endorsed Brands
Hydronex / HydroCore, Tempist Systems, and OpenLoop KSA operate as endorsed brands — they carry independent identities within a defined relationship to the Obsidian master brand. Each may develop sector-specific voice and visual accents, within the bounds of this document.
Tier 03 — Sub-Brands
Operational Marks
Product lines, service divisions, or joint-venture vehicles operating beneath a platform brand. These must be anchored by at least one tier-two endorsed brand name and may not reference Obsidian directly without tier-two intermediation.

Sub-Brand Relationship Rules

  • All platform brand materials must carry an "An Obsidian Capital Partners Platform Company" lockup — no exceptions in investor-facing or public contexts.
  • The Obsidian wordmark may not be smaller than the platform wordmark in any co-branded material.
  • Platform brands may not issue press releases, fundraising materials, or partnership announcements without OCP brand sign-off.
  • Color deviations for platform brands are permitted within defined sector accent rules (Section 3.4) — but the primary OCP palette governs all shared surfaces.
  • No platform brand may describe itself as an advisory or consulting entity. All language must reflect the principal-ownership relationship to Obsidian.
1.3

Brand Personality

Five attributes. Each with a behavior — and an anti-attribute to kill the cliché version.

01
Forged
In Practice

Communication that carries mass. Visual identity that has permanence. Every element earns its presence — no decoration without function, no softening for comfort.

Not This

Not Swiss-minimalism for aesthetics alone. Not "clean" as a style choice without substance behind it. Not polished to the point of being frictionless.

02
Precise
In Practice

Every number cited is defensible. Every word is chosen for specificity. No redundancy in language. No approximate language in financial contexts. Precision as a form of respect.

Not This

Not clinical. Not bureaucratic formality. Precision does not mean cold — it means exact, which is a different thing entirely.

03
Long-Duration
In Practice

Communication references cycles, not quarters. Brand language compounds on itself — later materials reference earlier ones. Visual identity is designed to age with authority, not become stale.

Not This

Not "patient" as a passive trait. Not slow. Not timid. Long-duration is an active, deliberate posture — not inertia dressed as philosophy.

04
Principal-Minded
In Practice

"We" language that reflects ownership orientation. Communication that assumes alignment of interest, not a client-service relationship. We speak as owners, not as advisors.

Not This

Not adversarial. Not extractive. Principal-minded does not mean self-interested — it means that our interests are genuinely and structurally aligned with the companies we back.

05
Kinetic
In Practice

Verbs that imply building, movement, and accumulation. Visual system has direction and momentum — never static or declarative. The brand is in motion. Always building.

Not This

Not frenetic. Not startup-urgent. Kinetic energy here is measured and directional — the force of compounding, not the noise of activity.

1.5

Messaging Pillars

Three load-bearing claims. Every piece of communication can be traced back to one of these.

01
We Build. We Don't Broker.

The advisory-to-principal distinction is Obsidian's sharpest competitive edge. Most capital platforms optimize for transaction volume. We optimize for company quality. The difference is visible in structure, in tenure, and in how we show up when a business faces difficulty — which is the only test that matters.

Proof Point
Principal positions taken across all active platforms. Obsidian capital is deployed alongside — never above — operator capital.
Headline Example
"We don't route capital. We commit it."
02
Structure Is Strategy.

At the growth capital stage, how a company is structured determines what it can become. Obsidian architects ownership structures, governance frameworks, and incentive alignment before committing capital. The deal architecture is the strategy — not a consequence of it.

Proof Point
Every engagement begins with a structure review. Advisory retainers are tied to milestone-based deliverables — not to hours billed.
Headline Example
"The structure of a deal determines the quality of its future."
03
Patience Is the Business Model.

Capital markets reward liquidity. Obsidian deliberately accepts illiquidity in exchange for compounding time. The businesses we build in Year 1 are designed to be stronger in Year 7. This orientation changes what we acquire, how we operate, and what we consider success.

Proof Point
Principal capital vehicles structured at 7–10 year durations. No pressure for early exit. Management fee and carry aligned to long-duration outcomes.
Headline Example
"Patience is not a virtue here. It is the business model."

Wordmark Brief
Structure & Rationale

The wordmark is set in Cormorant Garamond Light, tracked wide at +150/1000em. "OBSIDIAN" occupies the dominant line, scaled 2.5× the height of the subsidiary line. "CAPITAL PARTNERS" sits below in Montserrat Regular, tracked at +400/1000em, full caps. The serif-to-sans pairing within the lockup is intentional: editorial authority above, structural precision below. The scale contrast creates hierarchy without requiring any supporting graphic element. No tagline is used within the wordmark.

Symbol Brief
The Obsidian Mark

The symbol draws from the physical properties of volcanic obsidian glass: conchoidal fracture — the clean, curved break pattern unique to amorphous materials. The mark is a precision-drawn irregular heptagon, with one internal fracture line bisecting the form at a 23° angle from vertical, referencing obsidian's natural cleavage planes. The polygon is not symmetrical. It is not a gemstone. It is a geological event — precise because the material demands it, not because a designer imposed it. Rendered in solid black only (no gradients, no outlines, no 3D treatment). Works at 16px and at 4-meter scale.

Clear Space
Minimum Isolation Zone

The minimum clear space on all four sides of the wordmark equals the cap-height of the "O" in OBSIDIAN. For the symbol mark in isolation, clear space equals the full height of the mark on all sides. This zone may not be broken by any element — text, imagery, border, or color edge — in any approved usage context.

Minimum Size
Legibility Floor

Digital: Wordmark not smaller than 120px wide. Symbol not smaller than 20px. Print: Wordmark not smaller than 32mm wide. Symbol not smaller than 8mm. Below these thresholds, use text-only identification ("Obsidian Capital Partners" in Montserrat Medium). The mark is never scaled to a size where its detail is lost.

Forbidden Uses
01
Never use gold or yellow as a wordmark color on a white or light background — insufficient contrast and reads as decorative.
02
Never stretch, compress, or alter the aspect ratio of the wordmark or symbol in any dimension.
03
Never place the wordmark over a busy photographic background without a clear field or scrim.
04
Never apply drop shadows, glows, gradients, or any effect to the wordmark or symbol mark.
05
Never use the symbol in isolation without the wordmark in any first-use investor or public context.
06
Never recreate the wordmark in any typeface other than Cormorant Garamond — including "close" alternatives.
07
Never rotate the wordmark or symbol. The mark is always horizontal, always upright.
08
Never use an outlined or stroked version of the wordmark. Solid only.
09
Never place the wordmark in a box, circle, or any containing shape that is not part of an approved lockup.
10
Never use mid-gray (#6B6B6B) as the wordmark color. Approved colors are Parchment, Obsidian Black, and Gold only.

Typography System

Two primary typefaces. One tertiary. One type scale. Zero improvisation.

Primary Serif — Cormorant Garamond. Editorial authority. Used for all display, H1, H2, pull quotes, and positioning copy. Weight range: 300 (Light) through 600 (SemiBold). Italic used for pull quotes and lede text only.

Primary Sans — Montserrat. Structural precision. Used for all navigational, operational, body, label, and data text. Weight range: 300 (Light) through 700 (Bold). Never italic — direction, not decoration.

Tertiary Mono — Space Mono. Introduced for data tables, financial figures, technical documentation, and code-adjacent contexts. The monospace grid implies numerical precision and machine reliability — appropriate for Hydronex and Tempist platform contexts.

Iconography & Mark System

Stroke-based. Square-capped. No rounding. The icon language matches the brand: angular, precise, no softening.

Icon Design Language
Specification

All icons are stroke-based at 1.5px stroke weight. Fill is used only for specific filled accent icons (e.g., active states). Corner radius: 0 — obsidian fractures at sharp angles, not curves. Stroke cap: square. Join: miter. Icon grid: 24×24px with 2px optical padding. Scaling: always in 8px increments (16, 24, 32, 48px). Color: Parchment on dark surfaces, Obsidian Black on light. Gold used only for active/highlighted states.

2.5

Photography & Visual Direction

Photography is not decoration. It is evidence of the physical world our capital operates in.

01
Infrastructure

Industrial facilities, pipeline systems, water treatment plants, data center interiors, manufacturing floors. Dramatic single-source lighting with hard shadows — the physical substance of capital deployed. No wide-angle distortion. No HDR treatment. The frame should feel honest, not cinematic-for-its-own-sake.

02
People

Principals and operators in working environments — not posed portraits. Shot candidly in motion: reviewing data, in conversation, walking plant floors. Direct camera contact only in formal headshot contexts. No conference-room smiles. No diversity-performance casting. People who are visibly doing actual work.

03
Abstraction

Close-up material textures: raw obsidian stone, machined metal surfaces, aged paper, moving water, concrete. Macro or near-macro. No human presence. These images serve as section backgrounds and brand texture moments — they communicate material permanence, not concept.

Lighting
Single-source industrial or natural light. High contrast ratio — shadows are as important as highlights. No fill cards that eliminate shadow. Gold tones lifted in metal surfaces and warm light. Blue-black shadow tones in deep areas. The image should feel like it was lit by the environment, not a studio.
Color Grade
Desaturated mid-tones with warm blacks (slight amber lift in shadow). Highlights pulled toward warm parchment-white. Skin tones desaturated 20–30%. Gold and amber tones in metal, water, and warm light lifted 15%. No Instagram filters. No vignette. No color grading that calls attention to itself. The treatment should feel like memory — real but slightly distilled.
Aspect Ratios
Hero/full-bleed: 16:9 or 21:9. Section backgrounds: 3:1 cropped to center-weight. Portrait (people): 4:5 or 1:1 for headshots. Detail/texture: 1:1 or 4:3. Never crop below minimum recommended sizes — pixelation in brand contexts is a quality failure.
Avoid
Smiling executives in glass conference rooms. Generic city skylines. Stock handshakes and contract signings. False candid (staged "working" shots). Any image with visible brand logos of other companies unless cleared. Diversity performance casting. Action shots that imply urgency or stress. Anything that reads like a Big Four consulting brochure.
2.6

Motion & Animation Principles

Obsidian moves with purpose. Every animation is a consequence of something — never decoration.

Easing Philosophy

All motion uses smooth deceleration. Elements arrive at their destination crisply, without bounce or overshoot. The curve references the physical behavior of dense material — it accelerates quickly and decelerates with weight.

cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)
Timing Standards
UI micro-interactions (hover, click)150ms
Standard element transitions300ms
Section and page reveals500ms
Cover / cinematic brand moments800–1200ms
Staggered list/grid reveals60ms offset per item
Transition Style

Elements enter via fade + translate (Y: 8–14px up). Never slide in from the side. Never scale in. The direction is always upward — emerging from depth, not arriving from outside the frame. Exit is fade-only.

Motion Never

Infinite rotation, spinning, or orbiting elements

Bounce, spring, or elastic easing of any kind

Parallax scrolling effects on background images

Pulsing, throbbing, or heartbeat animations

Particle effects, generative noise, or ambient motion

Transitions faster than 100ms (unperceived = no communication value)

Digital — Website

The website is not a brochure. It is the institutional record. Every design decision reflects that.

Grid System
12-Column Layout

12-column grid. Outer margins: 80px at 1440px desktop, 48px at 1024px, 24px at 375px mobile. Column gutter: 24px fixed. Content max-width: 1280px centered. Primary content column: 8/12. Sidebar/supporting content: 4/12. Data tables span full 12 columns. Typography column (prose): 7/12 max-width (~700px) — never allow body copy to run full-width.

Spacing System
8px Base Unit

All spacing values are multiples of 8px. Scale: 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128, 192. Section padding: 96px vertical at desktop, 64px at tablet, 48px at mobile. Component internal padding: 24–48px depending on card weight. Never use non-8-multiple values in layout spacing. Margins and gutters are structural, not visual judgment calls.

4.2

What Good Looks Like

Five examples of on-brand execution. Five that are never acceptable. Memorize both lists.

On Brand — Execute Like This
A one-page investment mandate: no decorative elements, two typefaces, precise financial metrics in Space Mono, a clear structural summary, and a single gold ruling line. Reads in under two minutes. Answers every structural question without being asked.
A website hero section: obsidian stone photography in full bleed at high contrast, a single tagline in Cormorant Garamond Light ("We build companies. We architect capital."), one CTA button, and nothing else. No subheads, no bullet points, no paragraphs.
Business cards with exactly five pieces of information: name, title, email, phone, website. No social handles. No QR code. No physical address. Matte black, gold foil wordmark. The restraint is the message.
A platform brand deck that opens on slide one with the OCP parent relationship lockup ("A platform company of Obsidian Capital Partners") before any platform content appears. The lineage is stated before the offering.
A press release written in three paragraphs. No boilerplate about "leading" or "committed to excellence." No quotes from executives expressing excitement. The announcement contains only verifiable facts and a contact line. It reads like a primary source.
Off Brand — Never Execute Like This
An investment deck with 40+ slides, gradient backgrounds, stock photography of confident executives, and a "Our Journey" timeline slide. The slide count alone signals that the presenter does not trust the content.
The Gold color (#B8965A) used as a full background on any slide, card, or section. Gold is an accent. The moment it becomes a background it reads as a luxury brand, not an institutional one.
Cormorant Garamond used at 10px or below for body copy in data-dense documents. Below 13px the typeface loses the quality that earns its place in the system — it becomes illegible ornamentation.
A platform brand press release, website, or deck that makes no reference to Obsidian Capital Partners. Platform brands do not exist independently in the public record. The parent relationship is structural, not optional.
Website or pitch copy that uses "leverage," "synergies," "best-in-class," "world-class," "ecosystem," "disruptive," or "passionate." Each of these words is evidence that the writer did not trust the actual facts to carry the communication. Cut them.
4.3

Co-Brand & Partnership Rules

Obsidian does not subordinate itself to any partner brand. The relationship is always stated, never implied.

Alongside Investor / LP Brands
OCP and Capital Partners
  • OCP wordmark appears at equal or greater size than any co-appearing institution
  • OCP always uses its own palette — never adopts partner color or typeface
  • Separation: minimum 2× OCP clear space between marks
  • Placement: OCP appears left or above partner brand in any dual-brand layout
  • Never combine marks — both must retain full individual integrity
  • LP logos appear in grayscale in OCP materials unless co-brand agreement specifies otherwise
Alongside Portfolio / Platform Brands
OCP and Platform Companies
  • Standard lockup: OCP wordmark at top, platform name below, separated by a 1px gold rule
  • Platform brand color accents are permitted only below the gold rule separator
  • Platform names may not appear larger than OCP wordmark in any combined lockup
  • Minimum brand presence: "An Obsidian Capital Partners Platform Company" in Montserrat caption on every platform-brand page footer and investor deck cover
  • OCP may not be abbreviated (not "OCP") in any platform brand context — full name required
Minimum Brand Presence Requirements
The Floor

In every context where Obsidian appears alongside another brand — whether as parent, partner, or sponsor — the OCP wordmark must be present at minimum 60% of the height of the dominant brand mark in the composition. The wordmark may never be represented only by an abbreviation or monogram in investor-facing or public contexts. In digital contexts, at least one full reference to "Obsidian Capital Partners" must appear in the visible viewport without scrolling.

4.4

Brand Evolution Framework

The brand is designed to last. Evolution is earned, not scheduled. Some things do not change.

Triggers for Brand Review
  • Geographic expansion into a new primary market (beyond US and KSA)
  • Entry into an asset class that materially differs from current platform mandate
  • Major acquisition that creates a naming conflict with existing platform brands
  • Five-year scheduled review from version date (May 2031)
  • A reputational event requiring strategic repositioning
  • Merger, joint venture, or co-GP structure that changes the principal identity
Immutable — Never Changes
  • Primary color pair: Obsidian Black (#0A0A0A) and Gold (#B8965A)
  • Typographic pair: Cormorant Garamond + Montserrat
  • Core positioning: principal-minded, company-building, long-duration
  • The three messaging pillars in substance (if not exact wording)
  • The five brand personality attributes
Flexible — May Adapt
  • Platform brand visual identities (within OCP structural bounds)
  • Photography style and color grade as medium evolves
  • Individual copy and messaging for specific market contexts
  • Digital component patterns and interaction design
  • Accent color extensions (Forge Steel, Ember) — may be updated at review
  • Platform-specific typeface additions (e.g., Arabic for KSA expansion)
