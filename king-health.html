Brand Identity

The Brand

Brand Positioning: KING HEALTH is the performance-longevity health platform for Black men who treat their body as an asset. Not a clinic. Not a healthcare brand. A performance system — built by the greatest athlete of his generation, designed for men who measure everything.

LeBron James has spent 20+ years publicly optimizing his body — the diet, the sleep, the cryotherapy, the labs. KING HEALTH turns that philosophy into an accessible telehealth platform. The wealth-gap in health optimization closes here. What LeBron does to stay on the court at 40, every man in his community deserves access to.

Voice & Tone

Voice archetype: A trainer who became a doctor. Direct. Evidence-based. No condescension — the patient is already a competitor, treat them like one.

  • ✓ Say: "Your labs, your numbers, your game plan"
  • ✗ Never: "Wellness journey" / "self-care" / "holistic healing"
  • ✓ Say: "Performance metrics. Longevity data. Actual results."
  • ✗ Never: Clinical jargon or anything that sounds like a hospital brochure

Target Audience

Who This Is For

Primary Audience

Black Men 25–50

Fathers. Athletes (amateur to elite). Men who see health as performance, not weakness. Professional class and working class simultaneously — LeBron's audience is cross-income. Psychographic: "I invest in everything I do. My body is my most important asset."

Secondary Audience

Black Families via LeBron's Brand

LeBron's family narrative — "I Promise" school, Akron roots, father-as-role-model — creates family-health permission structure. Parents bringing children into the ecosystem. The gateway is men's health; the retention is family health.

Tertiary Audience

Performance-Optimization Men

The biohacker who doesn't use that word. Men following Bryan Johnson, Peter Attia, Huberman — but who don't see themselves in the white, tech-bro aesthetic of that world. They want the same rigor, from a source that looks like them.

Why LeBron

LeBron James is the most institutionally credible asset in this portfolio — and the most brand-safe. SpringHill Company, Uninterrupted, Liverpool FC ownership, Klutch Sports: he has demonstrated he can build businesses, not just license his name. His audience already trusts his judgment on health. Clinical Architecture

Health Categories

Hero Category
Men's Primary Care

Annual labs, metabolic panels, blood pressure, preventive screening. The entry point. LeBron's own health journey — documented in public — is the prototype patient journey.

Hero Category
Medical Weight Loss (GLP-1)

Framed as body composition optimization, not weight-loss clinic positioning. Performance framing: fuel management, metabolic efficiency. GLP-1 protocols through OpenLoop's Season Health partnership for nutrition integration.

Secondary Category
Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)

Wearable-connected health panels. Continuous performance data feeding clinical team. Hypertension, glucose, sleep, HRV. The "always-on coach" model LeBron already practices privately.

Secondary Category
Cardiovascular Health

Heart disease is the #1 killer of Black men. Positioning: cardiovascular health as performance longevity, not disease management. Risk stratification, lifestyle protocols, medication management where indicated.

Tertiary Category
Sports Medicine / Musculoskeletal

Recovery protocols, injury prevention, physical performance optimization. Serves both athletes and the much larger "weekend warrior" population in LeBron's audience.

Tertiary Category
Men's Mental Health

Positioned as "mental performance" rather than mental health — reduces stigma barrier to entry. Stress management, sleep optimization, performance psychology. Feeds into YZY HEALTH referral pipeline.

Clinical Infrastructure

All clinical protocols delivered through OpenLoop Health's NCQA-accredited network. 18+ OpenLoop protocols activated for KING HEALTH at launch. Clinician roster prioritizes Black male physicians — not as a diversity checkbox, but as the single most effective trust-building mechanism for the target demographic. "Your doctor looks like you" is not a tagline, it is clinical evidence for improved patient outcomes and retention.

Content & Trust Strategy

The Content System

LeBron is the most documented health-optimizer in public life. The content strategy treats him as the prototype patient — transparent documentation of his own health journey creates the trust architecture that lets everyone else follow. The strategy has one rule: real intake, real results, real clinicians. Nothing scripted beyond safety guardrails.

01

LeBron as Patient Zero

LeBron documents his own health panel — blood work, metabolic markers, sleep data, cardiovascular baseline — through a KING HEALTH telehealth consult on Uninterrupted. No scripted dialogue. Real intake. Real clinician. Real results. This becomes the brand's origin story and the proof-of-concept for the platform. Campaign runs Days 29–32.

02

Black Male Clinician Introductions

"Meet your KING HEALTH doctor" — a recurring content series featuring Black male physicians talking about why they practice in telehealth, what they see every week that primary care deserts miss, and the specific health risks that affect this demographic. Not recruitment content. Not marketing. Education delivered by the people who have dedicated their careers to it.

03

The Performance Protocol Series

Weekly short-form content on Uninterrupted and social channels: "what LeBron's labs look like at 40," "the metrics that matter more than your weight," "how cardiovascular health predicts your next decade." Data-forward. Clinician-validated. Built for the performance-minded man who has never had a doctor explain his numbers in plain language.

04

Community Health Events — Akron + LA

One in-person health event per quarter, anchored in LeBron's home communities. Free labs, blood pressure screening, telehealth onboarding. Not charity — a patient acquisition event. The bridge from community trust to clinical relationship. Target: 100 activations per event → 30 patient conversions per event at the 90-day mark.

05

Klutch Sports Network Activation

LeBron's athlete network through Klutch Sports provides a high-credibility secondary distribution channel. Athletes in transition (post-career health planning), younger athletes beginning longevity protocols, agents and team staff. A B2B2C play: Klutch as employer benefits partner feeding patients into KING HEALTH.

Men's primary care and cardiovascular management carry strong payer reimbursement codes. Post-enrollment in Medicare Advantage / Medicaid managed care networks, Care Tier effective cost to patient drops significantly. RPM billing codes (CPT 99453/99457) enable additional revenue per patient. Target: 40% of Care Tier patients in payer networks by Day 90.

FTC / FDA Compliance

Legal Architecture

Mandatory FTC Disclosure — All Commercial Content

"LeBron James is a co-owner of KING HEALTH and receives equity compensation. This content reflects his genuine experience and does not constitute medical advice."

  • GLP-1 content: zero compounding claims, zero sameness language, zero before/after imagery that implies guaranteed outcomes
  • Performance framing approved: "optimize," "improve," "support" — not "cure," "treat," "guarantee"
  • All LeBron health-data posts pre-cleared by KING HEALTH Medical Review Committee (48-hour turnaround)
  • NBA marketing rules during active season: consult with NBA League office before any in-season content activation
  • Patient testimonials: HIPAA-compliant consent required; no outcomes-focused testimonials without substantiation protocol
  • FTC Material Connection: Klutch Sports network activations require the same ownership disclosure as direct LeBron content
  • RPM data: separate consent layer for wearable data integration; not used for any content without explicit patient permission
Risk: Audience Expectation Gap

LeBron's audience expects elite-athlete content. The risk: primary care telehealth feels ordinary by comparison. Mitigation: never position KING HEALTH as "a doctor's office." Position it as LeBron's health operating system — the infrastructure behind the performance, now accessible. The framing is always performance-first, clinical-second.
